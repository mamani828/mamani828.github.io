About Me

I am a PhD student at the Department of Electrical Engineering at UC San Diego working with Prof. Reza Akhavian and Prof. Nikolay Atanasov. My main research interests area is in construction robotics, mainly focusing on safe navigation and learning from demonstrations for autonomous construction equipment.

Research

My research interests include construction robot autonomy. Specifically, I work on safe navigation and manipulator behavior. I have published papers in Automation in Construction and International Journal in Bifurcation and Chaos. I have also presented at ASCE I3ce 2024 in Pittsburgh.

Recent Projects

  • Ergonomic Optimization for pHRI with Reinforcement Learning

    This project focuses on using reinforcement learning (RL) to optimize human-robot interactions (pHRI) in a way that minimizes ergonomic risks as assessed by the REBA (Rapid Entire Body Assessment) system. By applying RL algorithms, the robot can learn to adjust its movements and interactions to reduce strain and improve safety for the human worker. This method circumvents the discrete nature of REBA and any other industry standard ergonomic scores which make gradient-based methods unfeasible
